Chapter 1: Introduction

Bipartisan support in America when it comes to viewing China as a 'strategic threat' yet no comprehensive and global strategy to deal with China.

First step to formulate any long-term strategy is to frame the right questions. One must always think the unthinkable.

Americans have long considered their country the strongest, physically, intellectually and morally. But can America lose?

On the moral aspect, increasing demonization of China, coupled with the thinking that good will eventually triump over 'evil' perhaps leads to underestimation of the odds.

America needs to adapt to this new world to preserve its primacy. Most Americans are quite unaware of the tremendous changes in the world.

Chinese civilization is remarkably resilient and Chinese people remarkably talented.

Renewal of Chinese cultural confidence and vigor.

Yet, it's unwise for Chinese leaders to underestimate the underlying strengths of the American economy and society.

Chinese culture values social harmony over individual empowerment. American culture is the opposite.
