2.6 The World Merchant Fleet

Ship types in the world fleet

In 2007, over 74k vessels over 100 gt. 4 main categories: bulk, general cargo, specialized cargo, non-cargo.

Bulk cargo: dry bulk carriers, oil tankers, combined carriers.

General cargo: container ships, ro-ro, MPP.

Specialized cargo: reefer, chemical tanker, vehicle carrier, liquefied gas.

Non-cargo: cruise, ferries, offshore, tugs, dredgers.

In 2007, about 8000 oil tankers, ships over 60k dwt mainly crude. Below, mainly products.

Tanker fleet, 5 categories: VLCCs, Suezmax, Aframax, Panamax, Handy (or Products) and smaller tankers.

  VLCCs: over 200k dwt

  Suezmax: 120k-200k dwt

  Aframax: 80k-120k dwt

  Panamax: 60k-80k dwt

  Handy: 10k-60k dwt

Bulk carriers, 4 categories: Capesize, Panamax, Handymax, Handy

  Capesize: over 100k dwt

  Panamax: 60k-100k dwt

  Handymax: 40k-60k dwt

  Handy: 10k-40k dwt

  Specialized hull: open hatch, ore carrier, chip carrier, cement carrier

Container ships: box holds and cell guides. Ro-ro: access by ramps. Multi-purpose (MPP): open holds, cargo handling gears but no cell guides.

Tugs: for pulling. Dredgers: clearing ship channels and sea floor.

Ownership of the world fleet

Must be registered under national flag, this is the legal jurisdiction, also subject to international conventions and laws of another country when in their waters.

Aging, obsolescence and fleet replacement

Sell and purchase market or scrap dealer. Shipping cycle is important.

2.7 The Cost of Sea Transport

World trade and the cost of freight

Cost of freight is now so cheap but varies in cycles. About 50 cents to $1 a barrel of crude long term.

Ship size and economies of scale

EoS very important. Capesize: coal and iron ore. Panamax: grain, coal and small iron ore parcels. Handy: minor bulks.

Size has historically increased over time.

Unit Cost = (LC + OPEX + CH)/PS. LC = capital cost, OPEX = operating costs, CH = cargo handling costs.

LC + OPEX + CH increase less than proportionally w.r.t tonnage.

Liners: many parcels, smaller sizes, need a lot of staff. Bulks: fewer parcels but much larger.

Bulk shipping economics

General principle of one ship, one cargo

Shipper can either run his own fleet (e.g. oil majors, big steel companies) or leave it to specialist bulk shippers.

Own fleet: can help optimize certain operations but huge capital investment. Some choose to just charter long term

Liner shipping economics

Cargoes too small to fill a ship on their own. Regular scheduled service between ports. A lot more complex admdin stuff so very high overheads.

Vulnerable to marginal cost pricing.

2.8 The Role of Ports in the Transport System

Port calls now highly automated, less obvious but a lot more intense and efficient.

Port: geographical area where ships load and discharge cargo. Port authority: provides maritime service. Terminal: one of more berths devoted to a specific type of cargo handling.

Secure location, cargo handling, storage for cargoes, integration with land transport system.

Lots of competition for ports in the same region.

Hong Kong, Singapore and Shanghai in the Far East. Rotterdam, Antwerp, Bremen, Hamburg in Europe.

Charge in 2 ways: allin or add-on.

The Shipping Companies that Run the Business

Types of shipping company

Some large ones: national ones like COSCO, China Shipping Group, Indian govt and MISC. Japanese trading houses like Mitsui OSK, NYK, K-Line, Korean shipping groups, independents like Maersk, Teekay, Ofer.

Who makes the decisions?

Wide range of corporate structures serving different needs and having different concerns.

Joint ventures and pools

Pools: fleets of similar vessel types with different owners and a central administration. Revenue-sharing arrangements. For ownsers, sort of like putting ships on time charter but with variable freight earnings. Owners usually still in charge of crewing, maintenance and technical stuff.

Pool manager: employment for fleet, collection of freight and payment of voyage costs, management of commercial ops, distribution of net earnings according to distribution key.
